1. Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

    FOR THE CAKE:
  • 1 and 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

    • FOR THE FROSTING:
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients
    • Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla to the dry ingredients. Beat with a mixer on medium speed for about 2 minutes.
    • Carefully stir in the boiling water (the batter will be thin).
  4. Bake.
    • Pour the batter evenly into the prepared cake pans.
    • Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Cool.
    • Let the cakes cool in the pans for about 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. Make the frosting.
    • In a medium bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the cocoa powder and powdered sugar, alternating with the milk. Beat until smooth and creamy, then mix in the vanilla.
  7. Frost the cake.
    • Once the cakes are completely cooled, place one layer on a serving plate. Spread a layer of frosting on top, then place the second layer on top and frost the top and sides of the cake.

2. Kulfi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional for extra creaminess)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios, etc.)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup khoya (optional, for richness)
  • A few drops of rose water or kewra (optional)
  • Food coloring (optional, for color)

Instructions

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat. Stir frequently to prevent it from sticking to the bottom
  2. Once it boils, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  3. Remove the pan from heat and let it cool slightly. Stir in the condensed milk, sugar, heavy cream (if using), khoya (if using), and cardamom powder. Mix well until combined.
  4. If using, add rose water or kewra and mix in the chopped nuts. If you want to add food coloring, this is the time.
  5. Pour the mixture into kulfi molds or small cups. Cover with lids or plastic wrap. Freeze for at least 6-8 hours, or until completely set.
  6. To serve, run the molds under warm water for a few seconds to help release the kulfi. Cut into slices or serve directly from the molds.
