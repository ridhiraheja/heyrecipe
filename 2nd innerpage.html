1. Paneer Butter Masala & Butter Naan

Ingredients

    FOR PANEER BUTTER MASALA:
  • 250 grams paneer, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup cashew nuts
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

    • FOR BUTTER NAAN:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour(maida)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water(adjust as needed)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons oil or melted butter
  • Butter, for brushing

Instructions

    FOR PANEER BUTTER MASALA:
  1. Blend the soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste. Set aside.
  2. Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  5. Add the tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add the cashew paste and mix thoroughly. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add paneer cubes and gently stir to coat them in the gravy.
  9. Pour in the cream and mix well. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.
    FOR BUTTER NAAN:
  1. Mix flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a bowl. Make a well in the center.
  2. Add warm water and yogurt into the well, along with oil or melted butter. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  4. Preheat a tandoor or a skillet on medium-high heat.
  5. Divide the dough into equal balls. Roll out each ball into an oval or round shape.
  6. If desired, sprinkle minced garlic or coriander on top before rolling.
  7. Cook the naan on the hot skillet or tandoor. Cook for about 1-2 minutes on one side until bubbles form, then flip and cook until golden brown.
  8. Brush with butter immediately after cooking.

2. Dal Makhani & Chapati

Ingredients

    FOR DAL MAKHANI:
  • 1 cup whole black lentils(urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup kidney beans(rajma)
  • 4 cups water(for boiling)
  • 2 tablespoons butter or ghee
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder(adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 cup cream(optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

    • FOR CHAPATI :
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour(atta)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt(optional)
  • Water(as needed)
  • Extra flour for rolling

Instructions

    FOR DAL MAKHANI:
  1. Soak the urad dal and kidney beans overnight in water. Drain and rinse.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked lentils, kidney beans, and 4 cups of water. Cook for about 20-25 minutes or until soft. If using a pot, it may take longer(about 1-2 hours).
  3. In a large pan, heat butter and oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste, followed by the pureed tomatoes. Cook until the mixture thickens and oil separates. Add turmeric, red chili powder, and salt.
  5. Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the pan. Mix well and simmer for 15-20 minutes. If it's too thick, add a little water.
  6. Stir in garam masala and cream(if using). Simmer for another 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro(coriander) and serve hot with chapati or rice.

    8. FOR CHAPATI:
  8. In a large bowl, combine whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until you have a smooth, soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
  9. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  10. Dust a rolling surface with flour. Take a dough ball, flatten it slightly, and roll it out into a thin circle.
  11. Heat a tawa or skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the rolled chapati on it. Cook for about 30 seconds, or until small bubbles form. Flip and cook for another 30 seconds.
  12. Press gently with a spatula or directly on the chapati to encourage it to puff up. Cook for a few more seconds until brown spots appear.
  13. Remove from heat and place in a container lined with a cloth to keep warm. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.
