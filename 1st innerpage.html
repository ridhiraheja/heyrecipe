1. Pani Puri

Ingredients

    FOR PURI:
  • 1 cup semolina(suji)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour(maida)
  • 1/4 tbsp baking soda
  • water(as needed)
  • oil(for frying)

    • FOR PANI:
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1-2 green chillies
  • 1-2 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 1-2 tbsp roasted cumin powder
  • salt(to taste)
  • water(as needed)

    • FOR FILLING:
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • chaat masala(to taste)
  • salt(to taste)

Instructions

  1. Make the Puri.
    • Mix semolina, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add water gradually to form a stiff dough.
    • Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
    • Roll small balls and flatten them into thin discs.
    • Heat oil in a pan and fry the discs until they puff up and are golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  2. Prepare the pani.
    • Blend mint, coriander, green chilies, tamarind, cumin powder, and salt with water until smooth. Adjust seasoning and consistency to taste.
  3. Prepare the filling.
    • Mix mashed potatoes, chaat masala, and salt.
  4. Assemble.
    • Make a small hole in each puri, fill it with the potato mixture, and dip in the prepared pani. Serve immediately.

2. Samosa

Ingredients

    FOR THE DOUGH:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water(as needed)

    • FOR THE FILLING:
  • 2 large potatoes(boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas(boiled)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Chopped coriander(optional)
    • FOR FRYING:
  • Oil(for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough.
    • Mix flour, salt, and oil. Add water gradually to form a firm dough. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling.
    • Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add potatoes, peas, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Mix well and let cool.
  3. Assemble the samosas
    • Divide the dough into balls. Roll out each ball into a thin oval. Cut in half. Form a cone and fill with the potato mixture. Seal the edges.
  4. Fry.
    • Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry samosas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve.
    • Enjoy with chutney or sauce.
